Robustness Analysis in Quantitative Management Research: A Review and Recommendations
2026-06-05 | DOI: 10.1177/01492063261440210 | Journal of Management
Authors: Shuai Yuan; Deanne N. Den Hartog; Yicun Liu; Annebel H.B. De Hoogh; Lujia (Sophia) Sun; Danqin Zhao; Katrin Riisla; Frank D. Belschak
Affiliations: University of Nottingham Ningbo China; University of Amsterdam Business School; Australian National University; University of Notre Dame; University of Warwick
Robustness analysis assesses the fragility or stability of empirical findings by testing whether research findings remain stable across alternative, justifiable analytical choices. Understanding the robustness of empirical results is important both for management scholars to build reliable theories and for management practitioners to derive appropriate practical implications for decision-making. Yet, the management field currently faces three problems in designing and reporting robustness analysis: terminological confusion, fragmented recommendations across isolated topics without integration, and a lack of systematic evidence on actual reporting practices. This paper clarifies the concept of robustness analysis and distinguishes it from related credibility-enhancing practices.
