Housing Wealth and Consumption: The Role of Heterogeneous Credit Constraints
2026-07-15 | DOI: 10.1111/iere.70086 | International Economic Review
Authors: S. Borağan Aruoba; Ronel Elul; Şebnem Kalemli‐Özcan
Affiliations: Department of Economics University of Maryland College Park Maryland USA; National Bureau of Economic Research Cambridge Massachusetts USA; Research Department Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia Philadelphia Pennsylvania USA; Department of Economics Brown University Providence Rhode Island USA
ABSTRACT We quantify the role of heterogeneity in households' financial constraints in explaining the link between the decline in aggregate consumption and the decline in house values between 2006 and 2009 using individual‐level data. Constraints that are triggered by a decline in house values, and a small fraction of consumers facing particularly severe constraints prior to the decline in house values explain 76% of the aggregate response. Local general equilibrium feedback and a decline in bank credit to consumers make up the remaining 24%. We find no contribution of a pure wealth effect in explaining the consumption decline.

Party Organizations and Complementary Political Control,
2026-07-15 | DOI: 10.1093/jeea/jvag041 | Journal of the European Economic Association
Authors: Ajay Shenoy; Laura V Zimmermann
Affiliations: University of California , Santa Cruz ,; University of Georgia ,
Abstract We study how political parties engage in political targeting of resources by coordinating politicians across multiple levels of government. We combine thousands of election outcomes from the Indian state of West Bengal with 300 million payments from a welfare scheme jointly controlled by state and local governments. We show that the state government gives disproportionate funding to co-partisan local councils, which target payments to raise votes for national candidates. Welfare payments are diverted to reward local councilors who successfully deliver votes, and to recruit opposition councilors. The ruling party recruits candidates only where it lacks a majority, systematically expanding its control.
