    Fair Allocation with Perishable Resources Food banks, blood banks, and vaccine programs routinely face a hard operational question: How can scarce supplies be distributed fairly and efficiently when tomorrow’s need is uncertain and today’s inventory may expire? In “Online Fair Allocation of Perishable Resources,” Sid Banerjee, Chamsi Hssaine, and Sean R. Sinclair study this problem in a setting where a decision maker must allocate a limited budget of perishable resources over time to stochastically arriving demand. The goal is to construct allocations that balance two competing objectives: fairness, where individuals receive similar allocations over time, and efficiency, where the available supply of goods is not wasted. Simulations calibrated to real-world data show strong performance and demonstrate that perishing-agnostic methods can perform poorly.
